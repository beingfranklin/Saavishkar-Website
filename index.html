Rules & Guidelines

General Rules

General Rules

1. Online registration is required. Registration is free.
2. Students may submit project as individual /group. (No of participants is restricted to 4)
3. In case of groups, all members must register together on one form.
4. A student can participate either in the mathematics stream or the science stream, not in both
5. All group members must be from the same institution
6. All group members should produce ID cards/Authorisation letter from the institution.
7. Each event will be considered as competition, only if the minimum number of teams is seven
8. Teacher/Parents /Guardian should accompany the participants
9. Participants and their parents and/or guardians must abide by all rules and other judgments set by the Committee. Failure to do so may result in the participant’s disqualification
10. Students are responsible for all aspects of the development of their entries.
11. Students must set up their project, remain with it throughout their judging period, and remove it at the end of the day.
12. Students must bring all necessary equipment, including extension cords. Outlets will be provided ONLY if requested on the registration form.
13. Commercially prepared kits or models cannot constitute a major portion of the project.
14. Students are solely responsible for the security and safety of their equipment.
15. Table exhibits only. Overall project size must be no more than 27 inches wide, 27 inches deep and 4 feet high. The entire project, including charts, labels, equipment, etc. must fit in this space. Use the ‘Contact Us’ link to submit requests
16. No hazardous & toxic materials are to be used.
17. Standard laboratory safety rules must be observed.
18. Prize money will be in the form of cheque in favour of the Head of the participating institution.
19. Decision of the organising committee will be final.

Objectives

Objectives

1. Stimulating interest in science, mathematics and environment and inculcating scientific spirit in young generation.
2. Exploring and encouraging scientific and technological talent among children
3. Inculcating in them a sense of pride in their talent
4. Making children realise the relationship between science, technology and society.
5. Understanding the need for proper management for the optimum utilisation of resources and prevailing technologies
6. Providing exploratory experiences, encouraging creative and innovative thinking and promoting psychomotor or simple apparatus
7. Encouraging problem solving approach and developing the appropriate technologies, especially for rural areas and integrating scientific ideas with daily life situations
8. Inculcating intellectual honesty, team spirit and aesthetic sense among the participants.
9. Popularising science and mathematics among masses and creating an awareness regarding the role of science and technology in socio-economic and sustainable growth of the country
10. Developing appropriate techniques for communication of science, mathematics and environment.
11. Creating awareness about environmental issues and concerns and inspiring children to devise innovative ideas towards their sustenance and mitigation

Criteria For Evaluation

Criteria For Evaluation

1. Involvement of children’s own creativity and imagination (20 per cent)
2. Originality and scientific innovations in the exhibit/model (15 per cent)
3. Scientific thought/ principle/ approach (15per cent)
4. Technical skill, workmanship and craftsmanship (15 per cent)
5. Utility for society(15 per cent)
6. Economic (low cost), portability, durability,etc. (10 per cent)
7. Presentation - aspects like demonstration, explanation and display (10 percent)
